What is Kapalmuks?

Kapalmuks is a Pepeton's specialty named after the Filipino slang for "thick-faced," as it is crafted from a whole pork face. Our rigorous preparation process elevates this humble cut into a premier culinary experience.

The Preparation Process

Making Kapalmuks requires expertise and patience. We take no shortcuts:

  • 1. Curing & Boiling: Seasoned with local herbs and sea salts, then slow-boiled until tender.
  • 2. Dehydrating: Hung to dry to remove skin moisture, ensuring the perfect puff during frying.
  • 3. Flash Frying: High-heat fried to bubble the skin into an ultra-crispy crunch while keeping the meat juicy.
  • 4. Serving: Carved into bite-sized chunks and served on a wooden platter with spiced native vinegar.

The Dipping Sauce

Our house-spiced native vinegar is brewed from local sugarcane and infused with siling labuyo (chilies), onions, garlic, and black pepper. Its sharp acidity cuts through the richness of the pork for a balanced, savory bite.
